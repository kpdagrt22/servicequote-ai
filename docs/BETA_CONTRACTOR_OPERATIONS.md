# Beta Contractor Operations Kit — ServiceQuote AI

Everything to validate with the first 5–10 contractors. Pairs with
`docs/DEMO_SCRIPT.md` and `docs/VALIDATION_PLAN.md`.

---

## 1. Recommended first niche

**Primary: Electricians.** **Secondary: HVAC contractors.**

Why electricians first:
- Quoting is frequent.
- Job scopes are understandable.
- Line-item quote value is clear.
- Many owner-operators.
- High value of professional proposals.
- Easier to demo with small jobs.

Why HVAC second:
- High job value.
- Urgent service need.
- Quote complexity is higher.
- May need a more accurate price book.

## 3. Good beta prospect
- 1–10 person contractor business
- Owner still handles quotes
- Website or Google profile exists
- Phone/email public
- Does residential service/installation
- Sends estimates manually today
- Not already deeply locked into ServiceTitan/Jobber
- Likely cares about professional quote appearance

## 4. Bad beta prospect
- Huge company
- Franchise
- No public contact
- Only commercial tenders
- Already uses enterprise FSM
- Requires insurance/legal approval
- Asks for exact guaranteed pricing automation only

## 5. Cold email script

**Subject:** Quick question about your electrical quotes

> Hi [Name], I noticed [Business] handles electrical work in [City]. I'm testing a
> simple quote tool for small contractors. You enter job notes like "install 6
> recessed lights and replace two outlets," and it creates an editable line-item
> proposal you can send to the customer.
>
> I'm looking for 3–5 beta contractors and I'll help set up your common price
> items manually.
>
> Would you be open to a 10-minute demo?

## 6. Facebook DM script

## 9. Pricing test
Test:
- $39/month Starter
- $99/month Pro
- $199 one-time price book setup

**Recommended beta offer:** "$39/month during beta. I'll set up your first 20
common line items manually."

**Alternative:** "$199 setup includes your price book + first month."

## 10. Questions to ask
- How do you create quotes today?
- How long does one quote take?
- How many quotes per week?
- Do you lose jobs because quotes take too long?
- What tool do you use now?
- Do you already have a price list?
- Would a branded proposal help?
- Would you pay $39/month?
- Would you pay $99/month if it saves 3–5 hours/week?
- What would make this useless?
- What would make this a no-brainer?

## 11. Success metrics

**7-day**
- 30 contractors contacted
- 5 replies
- 3 demos booked
- 1 paid beta — or 3 strong "set me up" signals

**30-day**
- 100 contractors contacted
- 15 replies
- 8 demos
- 3 paid beta users
- 1 retained after 14 days
- ≥ 10 real quote examples collected

## 12. Kill metrics
- Fewer than 2 replies from 50 targeted contacts
- Contractors do not quote often
- Quote accuracy concern blocks all interest
- They want a full CRM instead
- They are unwilling to pay $39/month
- Manual price book setup is too painful for all prospects

## 13. Feedback form
- Business name
- Trade
- City/state
- Quotes per week
- Current quoting method
- Average quote value
- Time per quote
- Demo usefulness (1–10)
- Would pay? (Y/N)

- Biggest concern
- Missing feature
